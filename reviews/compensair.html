What is Compensair?

Compensair is a claims service that helps air passengers pursue compensation after eligible disruptions such as long delays, cancellations, denied boarding, and some missed connections. On its official site, Compensair says claims can be worth up to €600 per passenger, offers a free eligibility check, and states that it works with 150+ airlines.

This kind of service is most useful for travellers who do not want to handle paperwork and airline follow-up themselves. The trade-off is that a successful claim usually involves a fee, and the timeline can vary depending on whether the airline pays quickly or disputes the case.

Key Features

  • ✓ Free flight eligibility check
  • ✓ Claims for delay, cancellation, denied boarding, and missed connection cases
  • ✓ Up to €600 per passenger in eligible cases
  • ✓ No-win, no-fee structure
  • ✓ Airline negotiation handled for you
  • ✓ May escalate to legal action when necessary

Pricing & Plans

You typically do not pay upfront to check or submit a claim. Instead, Compensair charges only if the claim succeeds. That model lowers the risk for passengers, but it also means you will not receive the full compensation amount if the case is won because the service fee is deducted from the payout.

Pros & Cons

✓ Pros

  • ✓ No upfront claim fee

  • ✓ Covers several common disruption types

✗ Cons

  • ✗ Not every disruption qualifies for compensation
  • ✗ Payout timing can take time when airlines challenge claims

🏆 Our Verdict

Compensair looks legitimate and can be useful if you want a hands-off way to pursue airline compensation. The main reason to use it is convenience, not magic: eligibility still depends on passenger-rights rules, and successful claims are reduced by fees. For many travellers that trade-off is acceptable, but it should be understood clearly before starting.
